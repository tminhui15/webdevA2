About

An amusement park is a park featuring a variety of attractions, such as rides, games and other entertainment-related activities. Meanwhile, a theme park is a type of amusement park built with structures that feature a specific theme and usually has many zones with various themes. This website aims to highlight on additional information of amusement parks and the risks of rides at these amusement parks.

Risks at Amusement Parks

Common Types of Amusement Parks Injuries

  • Head, neck or back injury
  • Stroke
  • Traumautic brain injury
  • Lacerations, broken bones or torn ligaments
  • Death

Common Causes of Amusement Park Injuries

  • Improper design of rides that engineers overlook during the design phase.
  • Mechanical failures such as track or safety belt issues, detachments etc.
  • Negligence from employees, not giving enough attention when operating the ride.
  • Passenger not following the safety instructions of the ride.

How Many People Get Injured at Amusement Parks Yearly?

Approximately 8,000 people get injured from amusement park rides in a year and these figures only include injuries severe enough to require emergency room care.

Safety Tips!

  • Don't ride if the ride appears poorly maintained or if the operator looks distracted.
  • Take breaks to prevent getting sick, hydrate yourself regularly.
  • Keep your head upright.
  • Do research on the amusement park, such as whether any accidents have occured in the past.

Accidents at Amusement Parks in the United States

1. Accident at Silver Dollar City

Silver Dollar City, located near Branson, Missouri, opened in the 1960s, welcoming guests to their 100-acre park featuring more than 40 amusement park rides. However, on the 9th of July in 1980, an accident occured. James Polley, a 23-year-old boy went on the Fire In the Hole rollercoaster. The workers were conducting a maintenance check and were unaware that Polley and other passengers were on the ride. They switched the ride's main track to the maintenance track, resulting in a low-hanging door where Polley did not duck in time, leading to serious injuries that led to his death.

2. Ride Derailment at Daytona Beach Broadwalk

On the 14th of June in 2018, passengers boarded the Sand Blaster roller coaster at Daytona Beach Broadwalk in Florida. The ride was fine until the roller coaster derailed, causing 6 out of the 10 passengers on the ride to sustain injuries. Two of the passengers fell out of the roller coaster, resulting in severe injuries. According to the investigation, the ride was operating at an excessive speed and the presence of significant damage to the track and axle systems was apparent, therefore, causing a derailment.

3. Ride Mid-Air Malfunction at Ohio State Fair

On the 26th of July in 2017, the opening day of the Ohio State Fair, an accident occured. The Fire Ball ride broke down in mid-air, leading to one death and injuries to seven people.
